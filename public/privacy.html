Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 8, 2025  ·  Last Updated: July 3, 2026
Your privacy matters to us. This Privacy Policy explains what data Lendie collects, how we use it, and your choices. By using Lendie, you agree to this policy.

1. Who We Are

Lendie LLC operates a peer-to-peer marketplace for renting, buying and selling items, and offering and hiring local services, accessible via web and mobile app. References to "Lendie," "we," "us," or "our" in this policy refer to Lendie LLC. Questions or requests can be directed to privacy@lendie.app.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Operate the Platform — create and manage your account, display listings, process bookings and payments
  • Facilitate transactions — connect Owners and Renters, manage booking requests, process and track payments and payouts
  • Send communications — booking confirmations, transaction updates, push notifications (with your permission), security alerts, and service announcements
  • Improve the Platform — analyze usage patterns, fix bugs, develop new features, conduct research
  • Safety and trust — detect fraud, prevent prohibited conduct, enforce our Terms of Service, respond to reports and disputes
  • Legal compliance — comply with applicable laws, respond to legal process, enforce our rights
  • Marketing — with your consent, send promotional content about Lendie features and offers. You may opt out at any time.
  • Future services — any new features (such as insurance, financing, background checks, delivery tracking, or subscription plans) will use data as described in updated notices at the time of launch

4. How We Share Your Information

4.1 With Other Users

When you list an item, your display name, profile photo, general location (city/area), and listing details are visible to other Users. When you book an item, your name and relevant booking details are shared with the Owner. Precise contact information (email, phone) is not shared without your consent.

4.2 With Service Providers

We share data with trusted service providers who help us operate the Platform, including:

  • Supabase — database, authentication, and file storage
  • Stripe — payment processing and identity verification for payouts
  • Vercel — web hosting and deployment
  • Future providers — we may engage additional providers for analytics, customer support, identity verification, background checks, insurance, logistics, or other services. All providers are bound by confidentiality obligations.

4.3 For Legal Reasons

We may disclose your information if we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to comply with law or legal process, protect the rights or safety of Lendie or others, investigate fraud or security issues, or respond to a verified government request.

4.4 Business Transfers

If Lendie is involved in a merger, acquisition, asset sale, or bankruptcy, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of any such change and your choices.

4.5 With Your Consent

We may share your information for other purposes with your explicit consent.

4.6 We Do Not Sell Your Data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for their own marketing purposes.

5. Payment Data

All payment card data is processed directly by Stripe and is never stored on Lendie's servers. We store only transaction metadata (amounts, status, Stripe transaction IDs) necessary to operate the Platform. When you save a card for a scheduled (delayed) charge, the card is stored securely by Stripe and Lendie retains only a Stripe reference token (customer and payment-method identifiers) — never the card number — so the authorized charge can be completed on the scheduled date. Owners who enable payouts through Stripe Connect submit identity and banking information directly to Stripe; Lendie does not store that information. Stripe's privacy practices are governed by the Stripe Privacy Policy.

6. Location Data

We use location data to show you listings near you and to help Owners set delivery radius. You can use Lendie without enabling precise GPS — you may enter a location manually. If you grant GPS permission, your precise location is used only within the app session and is not stored permanently on our servers. We store only the location you enter manually (city/area level) for search functionality.

7. Push Notifications

With your permission, we send push notifications for booking updates, new messages, payment confirmations, and other Platform activity. You can disable push notifications at any time in your device settings or within the Lendie app. Opting out of push notifications does not affect your ability to use the Platform.

8. Photos and User Content

Photos you upload for listings are stored in our cloud storage (Supabase) and are publicly accessible to anyone with the URL. Do not upload photos containing sensitive personal information. We may use listing photos to promote the Platform. If you delete a listing, associated photos are removed from public display, though copies may remain in backups for a limited period.

9. Data Retention

We retain your account data and transaction history for as long as your account is active and for a reasonable period afterward to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Inactive accounts may be deleted after 2 years of inactivity with advance notice. You may request deletion of your account at any time (see Section 11).

10. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your data, including encrypted data transmission (HTTPS/TLS), hashed passwords, row-level security on our database, and access controls. No security measure is perfect. We cannot guarantee absolute security and are not responsible for unauthorized access resulting from your actions (e.g., sharing your password).

In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

11. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correction — request correction of inaccurate data
  • Deletion — request deletion of your account and associated data (subject to legal retention obligations)
  • Portability — request your data in a machine-readable format
  • Opt-out — opt out of marketing communications at any time by emailing privacy@lendie.app
  • Withdraw consent — withdraw any previously given consent at any time

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@lendie.app. We will respond within 30 days. Note that deleting your account does not automatically delete transaction records we are required to retain for legal or financial compliance.

12. Cookies and Local Storage

Lendie uses browser local storage (not traditional cookies) to store your preferences such as dark mode, recent locations, and session data. We do not use third-party tracking cookies or serve advertising. If we add analytics or advertising tools in the future, we will update this policy and provide appropriate opt-out mechanisms.

13. Children's Privacy

Lendie is not directed at children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal information, please contact us at privacy@lendie.app and we will delete it promptly.

14. International Users

Lendie is operated from the United States. If you access the Platform from outside the United States, your data will be transferred to and processed in the United States, where data protection laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction. By using Lendie, you consent to this transfer.

15. Your State Privacy Rights

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, as those terms are defined under U.S. state privacy laws. Depending on your state of residence, you may have some or all of the rights described below. To exercise any of them, email privacy@lendie.app; we will respond within the time required by applicable law (generally 45 days). We will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

California residents have the right to: know and access the categories and specific pieces of personal information we collect, use, and disclose; correct inaccurate personal information; delete personal information (subject to legal exceptions); opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information (we do not sell or share it); limit the use of sensitive personal information (we do not use sensitive personal information for purposes that trigger this right); and be free from discrimination for exercising these rights. You may designate an authorized agent to submit a request on your behalf. As required by California Civil Code Section 1789.3, California users may also contact the Complaint Assistance Unit of the Division of Consumer Services of the California Department of Consumer Affairs at 1625 North Market Blvd., Suite N 112, Sacramento, CA 95834, or (800) 952-5210.

15.2 Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, and Other States

Residents of states with comprehensive consumer privacy laws may have the right to: confirm whether we process their personal data and access it; correct inaccuracies; delete personal data; obtain a portable copy of data they provided to us; and opt out of targeted advertising, the sale of personal data, and certain profiling. We do not engage in targeted advertising, sell personal data, or conduct profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects concerning you. Where your state provides a right to appeal a decision on your request, you may appeal by replying to our response or emailing privacy@lendie.app; if we deny your appeal, your state's Attorney General may offer an additional complaint mechanism.

15.3 Verification

To protect your data, we may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request, and we may decline a request where we cannot reasonably verify your identity or where an exception under applicable law applies. We will explain the reason for any denial.
